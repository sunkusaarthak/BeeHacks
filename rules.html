Rules


Rules To Remember

Eligibility:
The hackathon is open to all individuals who are interested in participating. There are no restrictions on age, gender, race, or educational background.

Theme:
The hackathon will have a theme or challenge that will be announced at the beginning of the event. All participants must adhere to the theme while developing their project.

Registration:
Each team must register online through a designated registration portal. The registration fee ranges from 400 - 600 rupees, depending on the number of team members. The registration fee is non-refundable and non-transferable.

Team Formation:
Participants may form teams of 2-4 members. Teams may consist of individuals from different locations or even different countries.

Submission:
Each team must submit their project online through DevPost by the deadline. The submission should include a video demonstration of the project, source code, and any other relevant files.

Intellectual property:
All teams retain the intellectual property rights to their projects. However, Bee Hacks reserves the right to use, reproduce, and distribute the projects for promotional or educational purposes.

Code of conduct:
All participants must adhere to a code of conduct that promotes respect, inclusivity, and fairness. Any form of discrimination, harassment, or cheating will not be tolerated and may result in disqualification.

Judging criteria:
The projects will be evaluated based on their creativity, functionality, usability, and relevance to the theme. The judges' decision is final and binding.

Prizes:
The winning teams will be announced at the end of the event and awarded prizes. The prizes will be distributed through hybrid platforms.

Disqualification:
Bee Hacks reserves the right to disqualify any team that fails to comply with the rules and regulations of the hackathon.

Liability:
Bee Hacks is not responsible for any loss, damage, or injury incurred by the participants during the hackathon.
